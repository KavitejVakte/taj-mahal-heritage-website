Architectural Brilliance

Why It Remains Unforgettable

Perfect Symmetry

From minarets to gardens, nearly every element is balanced along a central axis, creating unmatched visual harmony.

Marble Inlay Mastery

Rare stones were carefully embedded into white marble to form floral and geometric patterns with extraordinary precision.

Changing Light Effect

The monument's color shifts with the sky, from blush dawn tones to golden sunset and moonlit silver at night.

Historical Journey

Timeline Of The Taj Mahal

1631: A Turning Point

After Mumtaz Mahal passed away, Shah Jahan envisioned an eternal memorial that would honor her memory forever.

1632: Foundation Begins

Construction started with planning from elite architects and engineers from across the Mughal empire and beyond.

1653: Completion

The mausoleum, charbagh gardens, mosque, and guest house were completed into one unified monumental complex.

1983: Global Recognition

UNESCO named the Taj Mahal a World Heritage Site for its universal cultural and historical significance.

Today: Icon Of Enduring Love

It welcomes millions of visitors each year and remains one of the most admired monuments ever created.
